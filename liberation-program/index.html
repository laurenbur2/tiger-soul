Why This Journey

You were born a clean slate. Then you were programmed.

From our first breath we are told what is good and bad, how to act, how to appease — imprinted by family, society, media, school, and religion until we live on autopilot, run by beliefs we never chose.

Over nine weeks, the Liberation Program shines a light on that conditioning so you can see it, heal it, and release it. Your thoughts, emotions, and beliefs shape your reality — when you let go of the limiting ones and replace them with excitement, fulfillment, and joy, you take back control of your life and begin to create it consciously.

How the Journey Unfolds

Three movements, one transformation

Medicine is not the transformation. Medicine reveals the path — integration is how we walk it.

Week 5. One week together in person in Tulum, Mexico (November 15–19) — the ceremony itself, approached with reverence and safety, held in a sacred container with four weeks of preparation behind you.

Week Five · In Person

The Tulum Liberation Experience

Five days of ceremony, connection, and reflection at a private villa in Tulum — jungle, cenotes, and the Caribbean, November 15–19.

Day One · ArrivalOpening the sacred space

Arrive at the villa, settle in, and meet the group over a chef-prepared dinner. The Opening Ceremony follows: what called you here, your intention for the week, and the agreements that hold the container. We honour the land with an offering, and close the night with a traditional rapé ceremony.

Day Two · PurificationKambo, and a first meeting with Bufo

A guided Kambo ceremony in the morning, then spacious time to rest and let it settle. In the afternoon, teaching on Bufo and the ceremonial process, followed by a carefully facilitated introductory session with immersive sound healing. Pool, sunshine, and rest, then dinner and an integration circle.

Day Three · SurrenderBreathwork and the Bufo ceremony

Gentle movement to wake the body, then an extended breathwork journey to open the breath and steady the mind. The afternoon holds the central ceremony of the retreat, each person held individually, with unhurried time afterward to rest and reflect. Dinner and a second integration circle in the evening.

Day Four · RebirthCenote, Temazcal, and the sea

A morning at one of the Yucatán's sacred cenotes — ancient stone, jungle, and clear freshwater. Rest at the villa, then a traditional Temazcal by the Caribbean: heat, darkness, prayer, song, and release. We enter the ocean afterward to cool the body and complete the passage. Dinner, a final circle, and the Closing Ceremony.

Day Five · DepartureNew beginnings

Breakfast and a last morning together before goodbyes. You leave with practices, insights, a changed relationship with yourself, and the bonds formed here. The retreat ends; the work of liberation continues.

All ceremonial activities are optional and subject to individual health screening, informed consent, facilitator approval, and our established safety protocols.
