Website & Platform Development Agreement

Tiger Soul Medicine Retreats

This agreement is made on ______________________ between Lauren Burandt (the “Contractor”) and Blaine Mueller, Founder of Tiger Soul Medicine Retreats (the “Client”). It sets out the work the Contractor will perform, the compensation, and the terms of the engagement.

1. Overview

The Contractor will rebuild the Tiger Soul website and design and develop a connected platform, including a member portal, a staff operations dashboard, automated communications, and an online shop. The work is delivered in phases so the website can launch and begin accepting payments while the deeper systems are built out. The platform is engineered as one secure, connected system so that payments, memberships, scheduling, and communications all work together.

2. Technical Foundation

The following apply across every phase of the work:

•  Modern, fast, mobile-responsive rebuild •  Protected member accounts and data
•  Consistent, on-brand design throughout •  Staff-editable content and pricing
•  Secure, PCI-compliant payment processing •  Search-optimized, with analytics

3. Scope of Work

Phase 1: Website Rebuild & Program Pages

  • Complete redesign and rebuild of the public website
  • An individual page for every program, training, and package, with accurate details, pricing, and imagery
  • A designed service brochure for each program, training, and package
  • A complete menu of all offerings, presented in one place
  • Simple, secure purchasing on each page (deposits or paid in full)
  • Clear contact and inquiry options throughout
  • A membership sign-up path built into the site

Phase 2: Membership Portal

  • Secure member accounts and login
  • Ability to make payments and donations online
  • View programs the member is enrolled in
  • View credits and balances, such as coaching sessions
  • Access to included resources (documents, guides, and materials)
  • Member management of profile and payment details

Phase 3: Staff Operations Dashboard

  • Track upcoming appointments and the members they belong to
  • Track payment status (full, partial, or outstanding) for each package
  • Manage scheduling, calendars, and coaching sessions
  • Access member accounts to issue refunds, credits, or granted sessions
  • An overview of bookings, payments, and activity

Phase 4: Automated Reminders & Communications

  • Automated email and text (SMS) reminders for appointments and payments
  • Confirmations and follow-ups sent to members and clients
  • Alerts to staff for upcoming appointments and due payments

Phase 5: Merchandise Shop (built out in a later phase)

  • Product catalog, cart, and secure checkout
  • Order management and shipping
  • Developed after the core platform is live

4. Ongoing Maintenance & Support

Beyond the initial build, the monthly engagement includes ongoing maintenance, updates, monitoring, and continued improvements to the platform as Tiger Soul grows.

5. Compensation

$3,000 per month.

This fee covers the Contractor’s design, development, and ongoing maintenance work only. It does not include any third-party service costs.

5.1 Third-party services. The platform relies on a few outside tools and services, such as the payment processor, website hosting, the domain, and an email or text messaging provider (for example Resend or Twilio). These are set up under the Client’s own accounts and billed directly to the Client, and are separate from the monthly fee.

6. Terms

6.1 Term and renewal. This agreement runs in 30-day periods and renews automatically for successive 30-day periods, unless either party gives written notice not to renew before the next period begins.

6.2 Payment. The $3,000 fee is invoiced and payable at the start of each 30-day period, in advance of that period’s work.

6.3 Ownership. Delivered work and account access transfer to the Client as each period is paid in full.

6.4 Evolving scope. This agreement covers the build-out and ongoing maintenance of the platform. Broader services, such as a full marketing strategy, fall outside this scope and would be added under a renewed or separate written agreement, which may carry an adjusted fee.

6.5 Additional requests. If the Client would like anything else included in the build-out, they can let the Contractor know and it will be reviewed and added by mutual agreement.

7. Confidentiality

Each party will keep the other’s private information confidential, including member data, financial details, and business information accessed during the engagement, and will use it only to carry out this work. This obligation continues after the agreement ends.

8. Warranties & Third-Party Services

The Contractor will perform the work with reasonable skill and care. Payment processing, processor-level data security, and message delivery are handled by third-party services, such as the payment processor, hosting provider, and email or text messaging providers. The Contractor is not responsible for outages, failures, or losses caused by those third-party services. Except as stated in this agreement, the work is provided without other warranties.

9. Limitation of Liability

The Contractor’s total liability under this agreement is limited to the fees paid by the Client for the 30-day period in which a claim arises. Neither party is liable to the other for indirect or consequential losses. Nothing in this section limits any liability that cannot be limited by law.

10. Independent Contractor

The Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee, partner, or agent of the Client. The Contractor is responsible for their own taxes and provides their own tools and equipment.
