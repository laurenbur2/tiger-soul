About Rapéh

Grounding, clearing, deep presence

A sacred shamanic snuff carried for thousands of years across the Amazon Basin. Non-psychedelic, and profoundly moving.

Pronounced ha-peh · Non-Psychedelic · Ceremonial

What It Is

A master plant teacher

Rapéh (pronounced ha-peh) is a sacred shamanic snuff made from powdered Amazonian tobacco — Nicotiana rustica, also called Mapacho — blended with medicinal plants, ashes, and tree barks. It has been used for thousands of years by Indigenous tribes across the Amazon Basin, especially in Brazil, Peru, and Colombia, for grounding, cleansing, healing, and spiritual connection.

Rapéh is considered a master plant teacher and is treated with great respect by those who carry its lineage. While non-psychedelic in nature, its effects are profound and deeply transformative when used with clear intention in a ceremonial context.

Energetic & Spiritual

Stillness in the mind, ground in the body

Rapéh is revered for bringing the mind into deep stillness and the body into a grounded, centered state.

  • Clears stagnant or heavy energy from the energetic field
  • Silences mental chatter, bringing presence and clarity
  • Realigns the chakras and energetic centers of the body
  • Enhances focus during meditation or spiritual practice
  • Prepares the body and mind for deeper ceremonial work
  • Supports emotional release and heart opening
  • Clears the sinuses and awakens the breath

How It's Received

The kuripe and the tepi

Rapéh is blown into the nostrils using a special pipe — either a self-applicator, called a kuripe, or a tepi, used by a facilitator. The application is brief but potent, and may bring light pressure, warmth, purging through tears or mucus, or a gentle altered state of awareness.

The spirit of Rapéh works quickly and intuitively, going exactly where it is needed. Most people experience a sense of calm, clarity, and groundedness within minutes.

It is not about escaping discomfort. It is about creating space to sit with what is present, and to move it through with grace.

Who It Serves

Who can benefit

  • Those feeling energetically heavy or emotionally overwhelmed
  • People moving through major transitions — loss, endings, spiritual awakening
  • Travelers needing grounding after long journeys
  • Facilitators wishing to clear lingering energies from their work
  • Beginners seeking a non-psychedelic introduction to sacred medicine
  • Anyone seeking clarity, emotional reset, or deepened presence in daily life

Reverence

Respecting the spirit of Rapéh

Rapéh is not a recreational substance. It is a sacred medicine that carries the wisdom of the forest and the prayers of generations, and it asks to be approached with humility, intention, and reverence. Before each session it is customary to set a clear intention, enter a space of prayer or meditation, and allow time afterward for reflection and integration.

A note on care: Rapéh is not psychedelic, but it can be very moving emotionally and energetically. It is not addictive and is used ceremonially, never habitually. The tobacco is stronger than commercial tobacco and is used in small, sacred doses. Those with sensitivities or cardiovascular conditions, and those who are pregnant, should speak with a facilitator before use.
