The Reclaim Retreat

Come back to your truth

Four days in Tulum — ceremony, connection, embodied healing, and the kind of integration that follows you home.

Bufo · 5-MeO-DMT · Tulum, México · December 4–7, 2026

Apply Here

A Four-Day Immersion

Leave behind the noise of everyday life and enter a sacred journey designed to bring you back to your truth.

Set within the jungle, turquoise water, and ancient land of Tulum, the Reclaim Retreat is an intimate four-day experience of ceremony, conscious connection, embodied healing, natural beauty, and meaningful integration. Every element has been chosen to support a complete arc — arrival, surrender, embodiment, return — and to give you somewhere to put what you find.

Tulum · Quintana Roo

The Setting

A private villa, held by the jungle

For four days the villa belongs to us. It's where we sleep, where we eat, where ceremony is held, and where you rest afterward — no lobbies, no strangers, no need to leave the container to find a quiet corner.

Around it: jungle, turquoise water, cenotes, and land that has held ceremony far longer than any of us. We step out into it for the meals and the water, and come back to the villa each time.

The Itinerary

How the four days unfold

Every ceremony, meal, and moment, in the order you'll meet them. Nothing is rushed, and nothing is asked of you before you're ready for it.

Day OneArrival

Sacred grounding

Enter the space, meet your community, set your intention. Nothing is asked of you today but arriving.

  • Afternoon

    Arrival at the villa

    A private villa that becomes our home, our ceremony space, and our place of rest. Settle in, meet the people walking beside you, sit by the pool, and let your nervous system catch up with your body.

  • Evening

    Welcome dinner — Tierra at Holistika

    Vibrant plant-based food inside one of Tulum's most loved jungle wellness spaces. The first meal that tells your body the retreat has begun.

  • Night

    The Opening Ceremony

    Each person names what called them here, what they're ready to release, and the intention they're carrying. We set the agreements that hold the container.

  • Night

    Honouring the land

    An offering and a request for permission — of the land, the medicine, and each other — before any ceremonial work begins.

  • Night

    Rapé grounding ceremony

    A traditional rapé ceremony to close the first night: quieting the mind, releasing the travel, and setting you down before the day ahead.

The intention: safety, trust, and a body, mind, and spirit ready for what comes next.

Day TwoSurrender

The journey within

The heart of the retreat. The whole day is protected from the outside world so no one has to hurry through their own experience.

  • Morning

    Movement & nourishment

    Gentle embodied movement to wake the body and release tension, fresh-pressed juice and light food, and a guided practice to reconnect with why you came.

  • Late morning

    Sound healing & introductory Bufo

    Crystal bowls, voice, and percussion alongside a carefully facilitated first Bufo experience — met according to your own readiness and consent, never rushed.

  • Midday

    The sacred pause

    The villa becomes a sanctuary. Rest by the pool, journal, swim, or take a private facilitator check-in. Stillness is where the morning gets heard.

  • Afternoon

    The Bufo ceremony

    You're held individually while the group holds a field of presence and prayer. Nothing demanded, nothing promised — just the safest, most respectful room we can make for you to meet your own truth.

  • Evening

    Grounding & the integration circle

    Comforting food, a quiet villa, and a gentle circle for those who want it. Whatever you share is witnessed without analysis, interruption, or advice.

The intention: a protected space to surrender, release, and meet who you are underneath it all.

Day ThreeEmbodiment

Nature, nourishment, celebration

Bringing the experience back into the body, and back into life. We're not chasing another peak — we're starting to live what's already been shown.

  • Morning

    Fascia release & breathwork

    Restorative movement to reconnect with the body, then breathwork and guided reflection: what did the experience reveal, what are you ready to leave behind, and how will you live differently at home.

  • Late morning

    Conscious brunch — Raw Love

    Smoothie bowls, superfoods, and fresh plant-based cooking in a relaxed tropical setting. Nourishment as pleasure and self-respect, not discipline.

  • Midday

    Cenote Corazón

    A heart-shaped freshwater cenote in the Yucatán. Cool, clear, and culturally sacred — a place to swim, float, and land back in your body.

  • Afternoon

    Rest & private integration

    An unhurried return to the villa — rest, journal, connect, or take a one-to-one integration check-in.

  • Sunset

    The Tulum coast

    Bare feet in the sand and the horizon wide open — a threshold between who you've been and who you're choosing to be.

  • Evening

    Celebration dinner — Casa Banana

    A MICHELIN Guide–recognized Tulum institution: Argentinian influence, open-fire cooking, and an intimate jungle room for our final night together.

The intention: turn insight into embodiment, and choose what you're committing to.

Day FourReturn

Anchoring & farewell

Honour the journey, celebrate the connection, carry it forward. The retreat ends here; the integration is only starting.

  • Morning

    Farewell breakfast — MOTMOT at KAN Tulum

    Modern Mexican cooking beside a private cenote, hidden in the jungle. A last quiet hour together before the world comes back.

  • Morning

    Closing reflection

    One final reflection and one word for what you're taking home, then your personal integration commitment, a Tiger Soul keepsake, and a blessing for the road.

  • Midday

    Group photograph & departures

    One last photograph together, goodbyes, and transfers to the airport.

The intention: completion, anchoring, and a clear sense of what comes next.

The Full Arc

The four days are the middle, not the whole thing

The work starts weeks before you fly and carries on for a month after you land back home. All of it is included.

I The weeks before

Preparation & education

You won't arrive cold. Everyone is screened, prepared, and taught what they're walking into, so the first day in Tulum isn't the first time you've thought about any of it.

  • Health screening and intake review
  • A one-to-one call with your facilitator
  • Preparation guide — food, substances, medication, rest
  • Education on Bufo and rapé, and how safety is held
  • A group welcome call before you fly
II December 4–7

The four days in Tulum

Arrival, surrender, embodiment, return — a private villa, a small group, and fifteen held experiences from the opening ceremony to the farewell breakfast.

  • Opening ceremony, land offering, rapé
  • Sound healing and introductory Bufo
  • The Bufo ceremony
  • Cenote, coast, and celebration dinner
  • Closing reflection and keepsake
III The month after

Integration, together

In a few weeks you'll be back in the same rooms with the same people, and that's where this actually gets decided. So we stay with you — as a group, and one to one.

  • A personal check-in soon after the retreat
  • Group integration call, about a week later
  • A second group call at twenty-eight days
  • A personal thirty-day commitment plan
  • Ongoing connection with the Tiger Soul community

Your Invitation

This retreat is for people who can feel that something in them is ready to change.

For those ready to step away from the noise, meet themselves honestly, and reconnect with the strength and freedom that have been waiting underneath. If you've read this far and something in you already answered, that's usually the answer.
