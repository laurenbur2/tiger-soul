The Reclaim Retreat

Come back to your truth

Four days in Tulum — ceremony, connection, embodied healing, and the kind of integration that follows you home.

Bufo · 5-MeO-DMT · Tulum, México · December 4–7, 2026

A Four-Day Immersion

Leave behind the noise of everyday life and enter a sacred journey designed to bring you back to your truth.

Set within the jungle, turquoise water, and ancient land of Tulum, the Reclaim Retreat is an intimate four-day experience of ceremony, conscious connection, embodied healing, natural beauty, and meaningful integration. Every element has been chosen to support a complete arc — and to give you somewhere to put what you find.

The Arc

Arrival. Surrender. Embodiment. Return.

This is more than a break from ordinary life. It is time to step away from old patterns, reconnect with what matters, and go home with more clarity, freedom, and purpose than you came with.

  • A private villa in Tulum, ours alone for the four days
  • A small group, held closely from arrival to farewell
  • One-to-one Bufo ceremony with trained facilitators
  • Sound healing, breathwork, and embodied movement
  • Jungle dining, a cenote swim, and sunset on the coast
  • Preparation before you fly, integration for a month after

The Four Days

How the journey unfolds

Each day has its own work. Nothing is rushed, and nothing is asked of you before you're ready for it.

Day OneArrival

Sacred grounding

You'll arrive to a private villa that becomes our home, our ceremony space, and our place of rest. The afternoon is deliberately open — settle in, meet the people walking beside you, and let your nervous system catch up with your body. In the evening we gather for our first meal together at Tierra, inside Holistika.

Back at the villa we hold the Opening Ceremony: what called you here, what you're ready to release, the intention you're carrying in. We set the agreements that protect the container, honour the land with an offering, and close with a traditional rapé ceremony to quiet the mind and release the travel.

The intention: safety, trust, and a body, mind, and spirit ready for what comes next.

Day TwoSurrender

The journey within

The heart of the retreat, protected from the outside world so no one has to hurry through their own experience. We begin with gentle movement, light nourishment, and a practice to reconnect with why you came, then move into an immersive sound journey alongside a carefully facilitated introductory Bufo experience. Afterward the villa becomes a sanctuary — rest, swim, journal, or ask for a one-to-one check-in.

In the afternoon we enter the central Bufo ceremony. Each person is held individually while the group holds a field of presence and prayer around them. Nothing is demanded and nothing is promised — our work is to make the safest, most respectful room possible for you to meet your own truth. The evening stays quiet: familiar food, private support, or a gentle integration circle.

The intention: a protected space to surrender, release, and meet who you are underneath it all.

Day ThreeEmbodiment

Nature, nourishment, celebration

After the depth of ceremony, this day brings it into the body and into your life. The morning begins slowly at the villa — tea, fresh fruit, restorative movement, then breathwork and reflection around three questions: what did the experience reveal, what are you ready to leave behind, and how will you live differently when you get home.

From there we head out — brunch at Raw Love, then Cenote Corazón to swim and let cool water bring you back into your body. The afternoon at the villa is unhurried, and as the light softens we drive to the coast for sunset before our celebration dinner at Casa Banana. This is where the retreat turns from inner work to gratitude and celebration.

The intention: turn insight into embodiment, and choose what you're committing to.

Day FourReturn

Anchoring & farewell

The last morning is slow. After packing, we travel together to MOTMOT at KAN Tulum for a farewell breakfast beside the property's private cenote. Each person offers one final reflection and one word for what they're taking home.

You'll leave with a personal integration commitment, continued guidance, a Tiger Soul keepsake, and a blessing for the road. One last group photograph, and then the airport. The retreat ends here. The integration is only starting.

The intention: completion, anchoring, and a clear sense of what comes next.

Tulum · Quintana Roo

The Setting

A private villa, held by the jungle

For four days the villa belongs to us. It's where we sleep, where we eat, where ceremony is held, and where you rest afterward — no lobbies, no strangers, no need to leave the container to find a quiet corner.

Around it: jungle, turquoise water, cenotes, and land that has held ceremony far longer than any of us. We step out into it for the meals and the water, and come back to the villa each time.

Everything Included

Every ceremony, meal, and moment

Fifteen distinct experiences across four days — ceremony, movement, nourishment, water, and rest, in the order you'll meet them.

Day One · Dinner

Tierra at Holistika

Vibrant plant-based food inside one of Tulum's most loved jungle wellness spaces. Lush paths, art, and a slower pace — the first meal that tells your body the retreat has begun.

Day One · Ceremony

The Opening Ceremony

Each person names what called them here, what they're ready to release, and the intention they're carrying. We set the agreements that hold the container for the four days.

Day One · Offering

Honouring the land

Before any ceremonial work, we make an offering and ask permission — of the land, the medicine, and each other. Prayer and intention open the retreat properly.

Day One · Medicine

Rapé grounding ceremony

A traditional rapé ceremony to close the first night — quieting the mind, releasing the travel, and setting you down fully before the day ahead.

Day Two · Morning

Movement & nourishment

Gentle embodied movement to wake the body and release tension, fresh-pressed juice and light food, and a guided practice to reconnect with why you came.

Day Two · Medicine

Sound healing & introductory Bufo

Crystal bowls, voice, and percussion alongside a carefully facilitated first Bufo experience — met according to your own readiness and consent, never rushed.

Day Two · Midday

The sacred pause

The villa becomes a sanctuary — rest by the pool, journal, swim, or take a private facilitator check-in. Stillness is where the morning gets heard.

Day Two · The Centre

The Bufo ceremony

The heart of the retreat. You're held individually while the group holds a field of presence and prayer. Nothing demanded, nothing promised — just the safest room we can make for you to meet your own truth.

Day Two · Evening

The integration circle

Comforting food, a quiet villa, and a gentle circle for those who want it. Whatever you share is witnessed without analysis, interruption, or advice.

Day Three · Morning

Fascia release & breathwork

Restorative movement to reconnect with the body, then breathwork and guided reflection on what the experience revealed and what you're ready to leave behind.

Day Three · Brunch

Raw Love

Smoothie bowls, superfoods, and fresh plant-based cooking in a relaxed tropical setting. Nourishment as pleasure and self-respect, not discipline.

Day Three · Water

Cenote Corazón

A heart-shaped freshwater cenote in the Yucatán. Cool, clear, and culturally sacred — a place to swim, float, and land back in your body.

Day Three · Sunset

The Tulum coast

Bare feet in the sand and the horizon wide open — a threshold between who you've been and who you're choosing to be.

Day Three · Celebration

Casa Banana

A MICHELIN Guide–recognized Tulum institution — Argentinian influence, open-fire cooking, and an intimate jungle room for our celebration dinner.

Day Four · Farewell

MOTMOT at KAN Tulum

Modern Mexican cooking beside a private cenote. One final reflection, one word for what you're taking home, and your Tiger Soul keepsake before the airport.

Before & After

The four days are the middle, not the whole thing

The work starts weeks before you land and carries on for a month after you leave. That's what separates a powerful experience from a lasting change — and it's included, not an add-on.

Before Tulum Preparation & education

You won't arrive cold. Everyone is screened, prepared, and taught what they're walking into, so the first day in Tulum isn't the first time you've thought about any of it.

  • A health screening and intake review before you're confirmed
  • A one-to-one call with your facilitator
  • A preparation guide — food, substances, medications, and rest
  • Education on Bufo and rapé: what they are, what to expect, how we hold safety
  • A group welcome call, so you know the faces before you land
  • Intention work, so you arrive knowing what you came for

After Tulum Integration

In a few weeks you'll be back in the same rooms with the same people, and that's where this actually gets decided. So we stay with you — as a group, and one to one.

  • A personal check-in soon after the retreat
  • A group integration call about a week later
  • A second group call at twenty-eight days
  • Guided reflection and journaling practices
  • A personal thirty-day commitment plan
  • Ongoing connection with the Tiger Soul community

Your Invitation

For those ready to step away from the noise, meet themselves honestly, and reconnect with the strength and freedom that have been waiting underneath. If you've read this far and something in you already answered, that's usually the answer.
