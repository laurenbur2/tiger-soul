About Kambo

The cleanse of the forest

An ancient Amazonian medicine from the giant monkey tree frog — revered for centuries as a tool for cleansing, healing, and spiritual fortification.

Phyllomedusa bicolor · Deep Cleanse · Ethically Sourced

Origins

A sacred Amazonian tradition

Kambo is a sacred medicine used for centuries by indigenous tribes of the Amazon, particularly the Matsés, Katukina, and Yawanawá peoples. Derived from the secretion of the Phyllomedusa bicolor — the giant monkey tree frog — it has been revered as a powerful tool for cleansing, healing, and spiritual fortification.

In tribal traditions, warriors and hunters apply Kambo before expeditions to heighten their senses, increase endurance, and sharpen focus. Shamans use it in sacred ceremonies for energetic purification, and to strengthen the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.

The administration follows a ceremonial protocol: small points are made on the skin, and the secretion is applied directly, allowing Kambo's bioactive compounds to circulate and begin a profound cleansing.

The Science

The bioactive peptides

Kambo contains a unique cocktail of peptides that interact with the body's receptors. These have been studied for their potential to modulate immune function, reduce inflammation, and optimize neurotransmitter activity.

  • Phyllocaerulein — stimulates smooth muscle contraction, aiding digestion; carries analgesic and blood-pressure-lowering effects
  • Phyllomedusin — a potent vasodilator enhancing circulation and oxygenation; supports deep lymphatic cleansing
  • Phyllokinin — improves blood flow and oxygen transport; supports cardiovascular function
  • Dermorphin — an opioid-like peptide producing pain relief and deep relaxation
  • Deltorphin — modulates pain perception; improves stress resilience and emotional balance
  • Adenoregulin — aids neuroplasticity and cognitive clarity; supports restorative sleep
  • Sauvagine — regulates the stress response, easing chronic stress and adrenal fatigue

Ethical Sourcing

Respect for the medicine

Honoring the sacred origins of Kambo is essential. Ethical sourcing ensures the Phyllomedusa bicolor frogs are treated with respect, never harmed, and released back into the wild after collection. In indigenous traditions, Kambo is received with reverence and gratitude, aligning with the spirit of the medicine.

For those called to work with Kambo, it matters to do so with proper guidance, experienced facilitation, and a deep respect for the healing intelligence of this sacred Amazonian medicine.
